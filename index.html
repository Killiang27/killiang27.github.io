01 · About

I am a Mechatronics Engineering graduate from Lancaster University with a foundation in engineering first principles and a habit of taking projects from concept to a working build, covering mechanical design, embedded control, simulation and testing.

My most rigorous work sits at the meeting point of hardware and data: an LSTM model predicting the remaining useful life of lithium-ion batteries to within a fraction of a percent, and a vibration-analysis suspension concept for high-speed rail with machine-learning anomaly detection. Alongside my degree I completed two internships in live energy operations: Vitol on UK gas turbines, then VIVO Energy (Shell licensee) on LPG and oil infrastructure in Morocco, where I learned CAPEX/OPEX modelling and demand forecasting in the field.

That blend of engineering discipline and quantitative modelling is what draws me toward quantitative finance, which I will pursue formally through IE Madrid's MSc in Fintech.

02 · Projects
01
Third-year projectIndividual2025

Machine Learning for the Remaining Useful Life of Lithium-Ion Batteries

View repository

I trained a two-layer LSTM on the Stanford/MIT battery-discharge dataset to predict how capacity fades over time and to catch the knee-point where decline suddenly accelerates, landing a mean absolute percentage error under 0.07%. I also built a Gradio interface for tuning the sequence length, epochs and train/test split, with guards so the model never leaks data across battery boundaries. The same forecasting idea carries straight over to risk and asset-default modelling.

PythonTensorFlow / KerasLSTMTime-seriesGradio
02
Third-year projectGroup of 32025

Vibration Analysis: High-Speed-Rail Suspension with ML Anomaly Detection

View repository

Working in a group of three, I helped design a semi-active suspension for a high-speed train: variable-stiffness air springs paired with a hydraulic actuator and a secondary damper, all modelled on quarter-car physics. I then built an LSTM autoencoder that reconstructs the vibration signal and watches the reconstruction error for anomalies. When it confirms a genuine fault, the system tells the train to slow down on its own.

Quarter-car modelLSTM autoencoderSignal processingMEMS / piezo sensing
03
Second-year projectTeam2024

Line-Following Robot with a Dual-Barrel Ping-Pong Cannon

We built an autonomous robot that tracks a line with LDR sensors and fires ping-pong balls at a target once it has covered a set distance. The firing mechanism uses two 3D-printed barrels, an elastic-band ball hammer and a servo that releases a string trigger, so the two balls launch at different heights.

Embedded C / ArduinoLDR line detectionServo actuation3D printing
04
First-year projectIMechE2023

IMechE Autonomous Wall-Strike & Return Robot Car

For the IMechE challenge I designed and built a robot car that drives into a wall and then reverses back to exactly where it started. Getting it consistent came down to careful distance calibration and a simple control sequence the car could repeat every run.

SolidWorksDPDT controlMotor controlMechanical design
05
First-year projectGroup2023

Scalable Miniature Wind-Turbine Aerofoil

We designed, modelled and wind-tunnel tested a scalable aerofoil blade built to pull as much power as possible out of the airflow. The geometry came together in JavaFoil and SolidWorks, then we measured current, voltage and power across different fan speeds to work out the coefficient of performance and tip-speed ratio, along with where the experiment started losing accuracy.
